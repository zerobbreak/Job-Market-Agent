**BEHAVIORAL**

1.  **Question:** Tell me about a time you worked on a software development project as part of a team. What was your role, and what were the biggest challenges you faced?
    *   **Category:** Behavioral (Teamwork)
    *   **Likelihood:** High
    *   **Why it's asked:** Assesses ability to collaborate, contribute to a team, and handle challenges within a project.  Important for EY's collaborative environment.
    *   **Framework:** STAR Method
    *   **Suggested Answer:**
        *   **Situation:** Briefly describe a project (e.g., developing a web application for a university club, contributing to an open-source project).  Mention the team size and overall goal.
        *   **Task:** Explain your specific role (e.g., front-end development, database design, testing).

**TECHNICAL**

4.  **Question:** Explain the difference between a relational database and a non-relational database.  What are the advantages of each?
    *   **Category:** Technical (Databases)
    *   **Likelihood:** High
    *   **Why it's asked:** Assesses fundamental knowledge of database concepts, crucial for software development.  The job description mentions "General knowledge and experience in working with databases (Relational and non-relational)."
    *   **Framework:** Explain, Compare, Contrast
    *   **Suggested Answer:**
        *   "A relational database (like MySQL, PostgreSQL, SQL Server) stores data in tables with rows and columns, using a schema to define relationships between tables. They excel at maintaining data integrity and consistency through ACID properties (Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability)."
        *   "A non-relational database (like MongoDB, Cassandra, Redis) stores data in various formats like JSON documents, key-value pairs, or graphs. They are more flexible in handling unstructured or semi-structured data and scale horizontally more easily."
        *   "Relational databases are good for applications requiring strong data consistency and complex transactions (e.g., financial systems). Non-relational databases are suitable for applications needing high scalability and flexibility (e.g., social media, content management)."

On the back-end, I have experience with Node.js and Python (with frameworks like Flask or Django) for building APIs and handling server-side logic. I'm also familiar with RESTful API design principles."

6.  **Question:** Explain the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) and Agile methodologies. What are the key differences, and when would you choose one over the other?
    *   **Category:** Technical (SDLC/Agile)
    *   **Likelihood:** Medium
    *   **Why it's asked:** Demonstrates understanding of software development processes and methodologies.  The job description mentions "Basic knowledge and understanding of software development life cycle processes, agile development methodologies, software design and testing."
    *   **Framework:** Explain, Compare, Contrast
    *   **Suggested Answer:**
        *   "The SDLC is a structured, sequential approach to software development, typically involving phases like planning, requirements gathering, design, implementation, testing, deployment, and maintenance."
        *   "Agile methodologies, such as Scrum and Kanban, are iterative and incremental. They emphasize collaboration, flexibility, and rapid response to change. Agile involves short development cycles (sprints) with frequent feedback and adaptation."
        *   "The key difference is that SDLC is more rigid and plan-driven, while Agile is more flexible and adaptable. SDLC is suitable for projects with well-defined requirements and low risk of change. Agile is better for projects with evolving requirements and a need for frequent feedback."
